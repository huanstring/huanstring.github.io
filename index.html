Exploring the Safety of Speech Tokenizer for Privacy Protection
against Illegal LLM-based Speech Synthesis

Zhisheng Zhang, Zhiyong Wu

Abstract: Speech synthesis based on large language models (LLMs) has gained widespread use. While such techniques enrich and facilitate our daily lives, they also introduce harmful security risks when misused for malicious purposes, e.g., illegal telecom fraud. Existing defense techniques mainly focus on protecting at the timbre level, making the synthesized audio dissimilar while still preserving its usability. In this paper, we focus on the latest LLM-based speech synthesis techniques and propose a speech protection framework named ST-Guard. We introduce an adversarial prevention approach based on gradient optimization, targeting the speech tokenizer component, to prevent models from generating semantically meaningful audio at the semantic level. For the timbre protection, we extract speaker embeddings via the speaker encoder for timbre protection. To enhance the transferability of ST-Guard across different models, we incorporate an encoder ensemble approach and propose both untargeted and targeted protection strategies. Furthermore, we employ a masking effect method based on the psychoacoustic model to ensure that the embedded perturbations remain imperceptible. We conduct evaluations on four LLM-based speech synthesis models as well as one commercial black-box API model. Experimental results demonstrate that our method achieves better performance over previous approaches on both Chinese and English datasets, and ST-Guard also exhibits a certain level of robustness. The demo page is available at https://huanstring.github.io.
